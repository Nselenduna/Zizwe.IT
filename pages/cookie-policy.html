Cookie Policy

Last Updated: December 2025

Quick Summary: We use cookies and similar technologies to make our website work properly, understand how you use our services, and improve your experience. You have control over non-essential cookies.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device (computer, phone, or tablet) when you visit a website. They help websites remember your preferences, understand how you use the site, and provide a better user experience.

Similar technologies include:

  • Local Storage: Similar to cookies but can store more data
  • Session Storage: Temporary storage that's cleared when you close your browser
  • Pixels/Web Beacons: Tiny images that track website visits
  • Device Fingerprinting: Collecting device characteristics for identification

2. How We Use Cookies

These cookies are necessary for our website to function and cannot be disabled. They enable:

  • Authentication: Keeping you logged in between pages
  • Security: Protecting against fraud and malicious attacks
  • Preferences: Remembering your language and region settings
  • Session Management: Maintaining your session as you navigate

  • Traffic sources (how you found our site)

5. Your Cookie Choices

5.1 Cookie Banner

When you first visit our website, you'll see a cookie banner where you can:

  • Accept all cookies
  • Decline non-essential cookies
  • Customize your preferences

5.2 Browser Settings

You can control cookies through your browser settings:

  • Block All Cookies: Prevents all cookies (may break website functionality)
  • Block Third-Party Cookies: Allows first-party cookies only
  • Delete Cookies: Removes existing cookies
  • Incognito/Private Mode: Doesn't save cookies after you close the browser

Browser-Specific Instructions:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Apple Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

5.3 Opt-Out Tools

  • Google Analytics: Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on
  • Do Not Track (DNT): We respect browser DNT signals

5.4 Mobile App Settings

In our mobile apps, you can control data collection through:

  • In-app privacy settings
  • Device permissions (Settings > Apps > Revalidator > Permissions)
  • Ad tracking settings (iOS: Settings > Privacy > Tracking; Android: Settings > Google > Ads)

6. Cookie Duration

6.1 Session Cookies

Temporary cookies deleted when you close your browser. Used for essential functions like maintaining your login.

6.2 Persistent Cookies

Remain on your device for a set period (from hours to years). Used to remember preferences and analytics.

6.3 Our Cookie Retention

  • Essential Cookies: Session to 7 days
  • Preference Cookies: Up to 1 year
  • Analytics Cookies: Up to 2 years
